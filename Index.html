"The Boys" is a television series based on the comic of the same name created by Garth Ennis and Darick Robertson. The series was developed by Eric Kripke and premiered on Amazon Prime Video in July 2019. The plot of "The Boys" revolves around a group of ordinary individuals who decide to confront corrupt and abusive superheroes who misuse their power in a world where superheroes are beloved celebrities by the public. These individuals form a team called "The Boys" and embark on a mission to expose the truth behind the multi-billion dollar corporation that manages the superheroes and to curb their destructive behavior. The series is known for its dark, satirical, and often irreverent approach to the superhero genre, exploring themes such as corruption, fame, power, and morality in a world full of people with extraordinary abilities. It has been praised for its witty writing, complex characters, and solid performances.

"Dawn of the Seven" is a fictional movie within the universe of the television series "The Boys". In the series, "Dawn of the Seven" is a film that is part of the cinematic universe of Vought International, the corporation that manages and markets superheroes as celebrities. In "The Boys," "Dawn of the Seven" is promoted as the next big superhero movie, featuring the world's most powerful s uperheroes united to fight a global threat. The movie reflects Vought International's commercial and media-driven approach to capitalize on the power and fame of superheroes for their own interests, while also exposing the issues of corruption and manipulation that exist behind the facade of superheroes as icons of goodness and justice.

Corruption and lack of morality: Instead of being noble and altruistic figures, many of the superheroes in "The Boys" are deeply corrupt and lacking in morality. Many of them are involved in abusive, violent, and selfish behavior, and some even commit atrocious crimes without being punished due to their privileged status. Corporate control and commercialization: In the world of "The Boys," superheroes are corporate and completely commercialized. They are employees of Vought International, a multi-billion dollar corporation that controls and uses them to promote its commercial and political interests. This turns them into media celebrities and commercial products rather than genuine protectors of humanity. Dark secrets: Unlike traditional superheroes who are revered as role models, many of the superheroes in "The Boys" have dark secrets that the Vought International corporation tries to hide from the public. These secrets include abuses of power, covered-up crimes, and illegal scientific experimentation. Lack of accountability: In "The Boys," superheroes are rarely held accountable for their actions. They are shielded by Vought International and enjoy impunity to commit heinous acts without facing legal or social consequences. This creates a sense of injustice and hopelessness among those seeking justice against them.
